paneer makhani

ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 16 to 18 cashews
  • 400g tomatoes
  • 200g paneer
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 inch cinnamon
  • 2 cloves
  • 1 green chile (slit & deseeded)
  • 1 tsp ginger garlic paste (or ~4 garlic cloves)
  • 0.75 to 1.25 tsp red chili powder
  • salt
  • 0.5 to 1 tsp sugar
  • 0.125 tsp turmeric
  • 1.25 tsp garam masala
  • 0.5 tsp kasuri methi
  • 0.25 cup cream
  1. start rice in the rice cooker
  2. cut paneer into 1 inch cubes
  3. soak paneer in hot water for ~20min
  4. blend together tomatoes and cashews into smooth puree
  5. heat pan with 1tbsp butter
  6. sautee cloves, cinnamon, cardamoms, green chile
  7. add garlic/ginger and sautee until raw smell turns richer
  8. transfer tomato puree to pan
  9. cover partially and cook until puree thickens
  10. add chili powder, turmeric, salt
  11. sautee until mixture leaves sides of pan
  12. add garam masala and sautee for 1 to 2 mins
  13. pour 1 cup water and sugar
  14. cook until gravy thickens & butter comes to surface
  15. add paneer and kasuri methi to the gravy
  16. stir gently and cook covered for 2 mins on low heat
  17. pour cream and allow to bubble
  18. tranfer to bowl and garnish with extra cream and coriander
